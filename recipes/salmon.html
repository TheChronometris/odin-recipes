Pan Seared Salmon with Garlic Butter sauce

Here is a recipe I discoverd on Cafe Delites that I use as a baseline for preparing fresh salmon with any sides of choice. Their recipe suggests skin-off salmon fillets, but if you prefer skin-on fillets (as I do) then this recipe still works with no adjustments. This recipe is very simple and takes very little time to prepare and cook (10-15 minutes total), but is still packed with flavor. Be sure to have any sides you plan on having prepared in advanced. Below are the list of ingredients and step-by-step instructions to the perfect seared salmon and delicious garlic butter sauce. Hope you enjoy!

Ingredients

  • Salmon fillets (skin-on or skin-off
  • Lemon juice
  • Olive oil
  • Butter
  • Garlic
  • Parsley
  • Salt
  • Pepper

Steps

  1. Dry room temperature salmon filets with paper towel.
  2. Season all over with salt and pepper.
  3. Squeeze lemon juice over each fillet.
  4. Heat oil in a large non-stick pan or skillet over medium-high heat until hot.
  5. Sear salmon flesh side down, pressing them lightly so the entire surface of the flesh comes into contact with the pan, until crispy and golden.
  6. Flip and sear the other side until the outside is crispy.
  7. Add in the butter, garlic, parsley and lemon juice, stirring the melting butter around each fillet. (The butter will begin to brown slightly.)
